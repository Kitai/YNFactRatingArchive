Experiment Debriefing:


With this experiment, we're investigating various issues in how people interpret sentences embedded under predicates like `be happy', and `realize'. For example, you saw sentences like the following:

  1. Did John realize that Alice went sailing?

  2. Was John happy that Alice went sailing?

For both sentences, there is an assumption that Alice actually went sailing (which does not arise in a similar sentence embedded under `believe', e.g. John believed that Alice went sailing). Interestingly however, the sentences in (1) and (2) appear to differ with respect to whether or not it is possible to give a `yes'-answer to the sentence, and then deny the fact that Alice went sailing. Our research investigates the stability of this interpretive contrast between predicates like `realize', on the one hand, and `be happy', on the other.

The results will help us understand how such seemintly subtle aspects of word-meaning influence and constrain the possible interpretations of the meaning of a sentence.

On a more general level, this study will inform current debates on what factors contribute to different types of meaning in natural language, and how such factors are encoded in the linguistic knowledge of speakers. It will also help us to design further experiments to understand how words and sentences are interpreted in online language processing.
